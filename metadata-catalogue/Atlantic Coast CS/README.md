# Atlantic Coast Case Study Metadata

## Overview

This folder contains the metadata documentation for datasets collected in the Atlantic Coast case study of the DiverSea project. The study focuses on the Luiz Saldanha Marine Park (Portugal) and aims to monitor coastal habitats, biodiversity, and environmental conditions using a combination of remote sensing, in-situ sensors, and citizen science data.

## Study Area

Data are collected in the Luiz Saldanha Marine Park, located on the Atlantic coast of Portugal. The site is a marine protected area characterized by seagrass habitats, coastal sediments, and diverse marine ecosystems.

## Data Types

The Atlantic case study includes several dataset categories:
- Drone LiDAR scans – UAV surveys producing point clouds for seafloor and habitat mapping.
- Drone camera imagery – Orthomosaics and aerial imagery used for habitat distribution and coverage analysis.
- Underwater imagery – Photographic data collected during field missions.
- Side Scan Sonar data – Acoustic imaging used to map seabed structure and sediment distribution.
- Environmental DNA (eDNA) prototype data – Molecular datasets related to biodiversity monitoring.
- Temperature logger data – Continuous measurements of water temperature.
- Marine litter records – Observations collected through a mobile application by users.
- External biodiversity data – Data from the Biodiversidade PMLS platform and EMODnet datasets.

## Data Collection Timeline

Sampling campaigns are conducted during 2024–2025, with multiple seasonal surveys including:
- September 2024
- April 2025
- May 2025
- October 2025

Some datasets (e.g., temperature loggers and citizen science observations) may include continuous or ongoing data collection.

## Data Formats

The datasets are available in several formats:
- GeoTIFF files – orthomosaics and geospatial raster data
- Point cloud files – LiDAR drone surveys
- Image files – underwater and sonar imagery
- Small data files – sensor measurements and observational records

Typical dataset sizes range from kilobytes for observations to several gigabytes for LiDAR point clouds and orthomosaics.

## Potential Analyses

The datasets support analyses such as:
- Mapping seagrass distribution and seabed habitats
- Monitoring coastal biodiversity and ecosystem health
- Detecting changes in habitat coverage
- Studying environmental drivers such as temperature
- Assessing marine litter distribution

## Access and Sharing

Most datasets are currently available for free non-commercial use, with access managed through project partners or associated data platforms.
